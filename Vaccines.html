Vaccination for Mpox

Vaccines play a crucial role in preventing the spread of Mpox and protecting vulnerable populations.

Are There Vaccines for Mpox?

Yes, certain vaccines can help prevent Mpox infection, particularly for individuals at higher risk. Vaccination is a proactive step to reduce the transmission and impact of the virus.

Types of Vaccines

Several vaccines, originally developed for smallpox, have been found effective against Mpox. These include:

  • JYNNEOS (Imvamune or Imvanex): An FDA-approved vaccine specifically for Mpox and smallpox, suitable for adults and immunocompromised individuals.
  • ACAM2000: A live virus vaccine effective against smallpox and Mpox, used under special circumstances due to potential side effects.
  • LC16m8: A newer vaccine variant with a better safety profile, mainly used in Japan.

Who Should Get Vaccinated?

Vaccination is recommended for individuals at higher risk of Mpox exposure, including:

  • Healthcare workers and laboratory personnel handling Mpox specimens
  • Individuals with close contact to confirmed cases
  • Communities experiencing outbreaks or high transmission rates

Vaccine Effectiveness

Vaccines are most effective when administered before exposure to the virus. However, they can still provide protection if given shortly after exposure. Key benefits include:

  • Reducing the severity of symptoms if infection occurs
  • Lowering the risk of transmission within communities
  • Providing long-term immunity against Mpox and related viruses

Side Effects and Safety

Most vaccines are safe, with minimal side effects. Common side effects include:

  • Pain or swelling at the injection site
  • Fever and fatigue
  • Headache or muscle aches
