Transmission of Mpox

Understanding how Mpox spreads is crucial for its prevention and control.

How Does Mpox Spread?

Mpox can spread through direct contact with an infected person, exposure to contaminated surfaces, or through respiratory droplets. Knowing the transmission modes can help in taking preventive measures.

Human-to-Human Transmission

Mpox primarily spreads through close contact. Common ways it can be transmitted between individuals include:

  • Direct contact with bodily fluids or lesions of an infected person
  • Respiratory droplets from prolonged face-to-face interaction
  • Touching contaminated surfaces, objects, or clothing

Animal-to-Human Transmission

Mpox is a zoonotic disease, meaning it can also spread from animals to humans. Key transmission routes include:

  • Bites or scratches from infected animals
  • Handling or consuming contaminated animal products
  • Contact with animal fluids or waste

Surface and Object Transmission

Mpox can survive on surfaces and objects for a period, leading to transmission through:

  • Touching contaminated doorknobs, utensils, or electronic devices
  • Sharing personal items like towels, bedding, or clothing

How to Reduce Transmission Risk

To minimize the risk of Mpox transmission, consider adopting the following preventive measures:

  • Maintain good hand hygiene by washing hands frequently with soap and water
  • Avoid close contact with infected individuals or animals
  • Use personal protective equipment (PPE) when necessary
  • Disinfect frequently touched surfaces regularly

When to Seek Medical Help

If you suspect exposure to Mpox or exhibit symptoms, it's essential to seek medical advice promptly to prevent further spread.
